## **Market Opportunity**

### **Industry Tailwinds**

The U.S. trades industry is experiencing a multi-decade growth cycle driven by converging structural forces:

**Aging infrastructure:** Population growth coupled with aging buildings, electrical systems, and mechanical infrastructure is creating sustained demand for service and replacement work. This is a 20–30 year cycle before demand compression.

**Data center expansion:** Hyperscaler buildout (AWS, Azure, GCP) is driving massive electrical contracting demand. 50–60% of data center capital expenditure flows directly to electrical contractors.

**Clean energy transition:** Government incentives for heat pumps, solar, and electrification are expanding HVAC and electrical service volumes across residential and commercial segments.

**Labor supply gap:** A persistent shortage of skilled technicians is creating pricing power for well-managed operators and raising the premium on efficient labor utilization.

### **The Technology Gap**

There is a massive gap between the theoretical capabilities of AI and automation and actual implementation in the trades. Over 90% of trades companies have not meaningfully adopted AI or advanced automation tools. Operators are anxious about AI innovation but lack any prioritization framework for adoption. Trades businesses are bombarded by 15–20 software vendors weekly, creating decision fatigue and vendor skepticism. Implementations of even existing platforms like ServiceTitan take 3+ months and require extensive hand-holding.

This creates an enormous opportunity for a trusted partner who can cut through the noise, diagnose actual needs, and deliver working solutions quickly.

### **Valuation Arbitrage**

Within the trades, there is a significant valuation gap that creates strategic opportunity. HVAC and plumbing businesses currently trade at 9–12x EBITDA. Commercial electrical contractors trade at 6–8x EBITDA despite arguably greater long-term importance (data centers, infrastructure). Specialty and commercial contractors trade at a discount because investors are less familiar with their revenue scaling playbooks.

This arbitrage is the foundation of LogBrief's longer-term platform strategy: prove value creation capability through the services business, then selectively acquire undervalued operators.

## **Case Study: Skycrest Roofing**

*Initial validation engagement — April 2026*

### **Company Overview**

Skycrest Roofing operates across Colorado and Florida with approximately $2.5M in average monthly revenue. The Colorado business is PE-backed (Shore Capital), storm-driven, and performing well. The Florida business serves an older demographic (median age 72\) in a non-storm market and is facing significant operational challenges.

### **Pain Points Identified**

**Follow-up conversion gap:** SQL-to-sale conversion sits at 30–40% with potential to reach 40–45%. An additional 5% monthly improvement represents \~$125K in incremental revenue. Account managers are strong on initial contact but struggle with persistent follow-up on stale leads.

**Unsold estimate backlog:** $18M in unconverted estimates accumulated over 4 years. Previous recapture attempts (call center, email blasts) failed. Text messaging showed promise but hasn't been systematically deployed.

**Off-hours scheduling bottleneck:** AnswerForce handles after-hours calls and collects full customer information, but cannot book appointments directly into AccuLynx. This creates a delay that loses customers who are ready to schedule.

**Referral program underperformance:** Currently using Snowball for automated referrals ($500 customer incentive for reroofs), but adoption has been disappointing. Account managers are not consistently asking for referrals at the optimal moment (post-job completion 'warm glow').

**Conversion paradox:** As marketing has become more professionalized, MQL-to-SQL conversion has dropped. With no marketing, the business converted \~80% of leads. Broader, less-qualified lead pools are diluting conversion rates.

### **Proposed Solutions**

**AI scheduling agent:** Rules-based engine that accesses AccuLynx calendar via single API license, checks zip code territory assignments, and books appointments directly — eliminating the AnswerForce-to-scheduler handoff. Expected impact: 5–10% appointment increase, translating to thousands in monthly revenue.

**Automated follow-up sequences:** Text-based follow-up workflows for leads that go cold after initial contact. Integrates with existing CRM to trigger re-engagement at defined intervals.

**Referral engine optimization:** Custom referral landing pages with attribution tracking, post-job automated referral requests timed to the 'warm glow' moment, and data cleanup of customer records back to January 2025\.

## **Key Risks & Mitigants**

**Willingness to pay** — Trades businesses historically resistant to software/consulting spend. Mitigant: Money-back guarantee; outcomes-based pricing; target PE-backed companies with allocated budgets.

**Market saturation of AI pitches** — Trades operators fatigued by 15–20 vendor pitches per week. Mitigant: Position as advocate, not vendor; platform-agnostic; lead with audit, not product.

**Execution complexity** — Custom implementations may take longer than 2-week target. Mitigant: Prioritize off-the-shelf solutions; build reusable product corpus; only take custom projects that generalize.

**Competitive landscape** — ServiceTitan ($3B+ valuation) and others have massive head starts in pure software. Mitigant: We are not competing on software — we compete on implementation \+ advisory \+ outcomes. Complementary, not competitive.

**Acquisition risk (long-term)** — Roll-up strategy requires deal flow, diligence, and operational bandwidth. Mitigant: Sequence — prove value creation through services first, then pursue acquisitions with demonstrated playbook.
